## The Old Testament Saints and Abraham's Bosom

The Old Testament saints were men and women waiting for the promise of the cross to be fulfilled in them when God would come, take away their whole nature, and give them anew that freed them from sin. For thousands of years the Old Testament saints waited, lived, and died, and went to a place called Abraham's bosom, an Old Testament type of heaven, and waited.

It was a very comfortable place that God had provided for them while waiting out the different dispensations leading up to the cross when God would be able to give them a new nature and once and for all free them from sin and finally make it possible for them to leave the shadows and types of the old covenant behind, including the Old Testament type of heaven, Abraham's bosom, and go into heaven itself, into the presence of God.

Luke 16, 22 briefly mentions this place when he says, "And it came to pass that beggar died and was carried away into Abraham's bosom." And you know the term Abraham's bosom was talking about everyone who embraced the promise God had made to Abraham that in his seed, which was Christ, would all the nations of the earth be blessed.

And anyone who died resting in that promise waited in Abraham's bosom until the waiting period was over and the Old Testament saints could be born again, receive a new nature, and once again dwell in the presence of God. And that had not been since the fall of Adam and the spiritual death of the human race.
